# Executive Summary: Local vs Foreign Eaters in OC Cities

## Answer to Question: "Is the increase in eaters due to foreigners or local?"

---

## **PRIMARY ANSWER: LOCAL (89.8%)**

The increase in eaters for Outer Cities (OC) is **primarily driven by LOCAL MALAYSIANS (89.8%)**, not foreigners. 

**Breakdown:**
- **Local Eaters**: +63,640 eaters (+3.9% growth) = **89.8% of total growth**
- **Foreign Eaters**: +6,311 eaters (+5.1% growth) = **8.9% of total growth**
- **Total Growth**: +70,862 eaters

## Strategic Implications

### Primary Strategy: Local Eater Acquisition
**89.8% of growth comes from local Malaysians**
- ✅ Current local acquisition campaigns are effective
- ✅ Continue and intensify local marketing
- ✅ Focus on cities with high local growth (Melaka, Kuching, Kota Kinabalu)

### Secondary Strategy: Foreign Eater Opportunity
**8.9% of growth from foreign eaters, but higher value**
- ✅ Foreign eaters show higher basket size (MYR 41.60 vs MYR 33.04)
- ✅ Target tourism segment, especially East Asian countries
- ✅ Focus on tourist-friendly cities (Penang, Johor Bahru)
- ✅ China market shows highest growth rate (+19.8%)

---

## Conclusion

**The increase in OC cities eaters is PRIMARILY DRIVEN BY LOCAL MALAYSIANS (89.8%), not foreigners.**

While foreign eaters show a higher growth rate (5.1% vs 3.9%) and higher basket size, local eaters drive the absolute growth due to their much larger base (13.1x larger).

**Recommendation**: Continue focusing on local Malaysian eater acquisition as the primary strategy, while leveraging foreign eater opportunities in tourist-friendly cities.
